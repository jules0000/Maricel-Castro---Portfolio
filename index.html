    When I was in college, I was hoping to take the architecture course, but my Yoyo, my adoptive parent asked me to take Education instead since there were no teachers in the family.

    Now I come to realize, I guess teaching really is my calling. For more than 3 decades, I was able to impart the skills, knowledge, experiences, and learnings that I have, not just in the four corners of the classroom, but also to a lot of students, children, colleagues, parents, and the community by which they were able to bring as they grew older.

    During my tenure in Binitayan Elementary School, there was not much of activities that honed the talents and skills of its students. The school doesn't participate much in programs outside its bounds & wasn't well-known for talents coming from the school. I understood why because most of the students, even with talent, didn't have the means to join or participate in activities. Some students were also part of the indigenous community, but it didn't stop me from helping these kids with a lot of potential. By then, I started to coach the Drum & Lyre Corps. I also started looking for great dancers and we started the Dance Troupe-BES Warriors/Binitayan Dance Troupe/Pepperezzi, PEP Squad, and then the Gymnastics Team. Regardless of their background, as long as they can perform well, I took a chance on these kids and believed in their capabilities. There was a lot of potential coming from the student body. There were a lot of good children who were not just good in academics but also in physical activities. There were a lot of times that we were not supported by the school financially & morally, and I have to depend on my income to help my students because I believe that these kids could do more. By then, we were able to join a lot of competitions. Our Dance Troupe and PEP Squad were always the defending champions in competitions like Juicy Inter-School Competition, Cheerdance & Pep Squad Competitions, Pastores Competitions and etcetera.

    In gymnastics, we only started with improvised equipments,. It was a struggle for us to practice since we do not have the equipment for training. We improvised a lot & my athletes trained until nighttime especially when competitions are coming up. By the year 2016, I was able to bring my student to the Nationals.

    By then, Binitayan Elementary School became well-known for very young & well-trained dancers/performers and athletes. Even the older performers, they felt intimidated in competitions seeing our kids performing on stage as they well knew that my dancers were very skilled as well & can keep up with them. As my student athletes/dancers grew older, doors & opportunities opened for them as they already had the skills, confidence to step up.
